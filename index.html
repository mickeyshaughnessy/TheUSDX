US Federal Data Exchange

A FOIA-compliant interface for individual-level government records. Submit natural-language queries — our AI matches the right dataset and automatically applies two-tier redaction before returning results.

Two-tier FOIA redaction (5 U.S.C. § 552): Classified, statutory, and operationally sensitive fields are blind-redacted with [b(Ex.N)] markers citing the applicable exemption — e.g. SSNs become [b(Ex.3)], clearance levels become [b(Ex.1)]. Personal privacy fields (names, addresses, dates of birth, phone numbers) are smart-redacted — replaced with realistic equivalents so record structure is preserved: "Marcus Thompson" becomes "David Ellison", "412-67-8234" becomes [b(Ex.3)]. All non-exempt fields — case numbers, dates, titles, pay grades, dispositions — are released in full per the FOIA segregability requirement.

                        Blind redaction — value suppressed entirely.
                        Ex.1: classified national security info (markings, clearance levels, covert facilities).
                        Ex.3: statutorily protected (SSNs under CIA Act; intelligence program identifiers).
                        Ex.7(F): information that could endanger life or safety.
                    

                    

                        David Ellison
                        Smart redaction — realistic substitute value.
                        Ex.6: personal privacy (names, addresses, DOB, phone numbers replaced with
                        different plausible equivalents).
                        Ex.7(C): third-party names in law enforcement records.
                    

                    

                        CIA-HC-2019-04371
                        Released — non-exempt, segregable fields preserved:
                        case/employee IDs, event dates, position titles, pay grades,
                        salary amounts, complaint categories, disposition summaries.
